[Refrain]
T: Gaan gai amar mon re bujhai
B: গান গাই আমার মন রে বুঝাই
E: I sing to make sense of my own heart

T: Mon thake pagolpara
B: মন থাকে পাগলপারা
E: The mind stays wild and uncontrollable

T: Aar kichhu chay na mone
B: আর কিছু চায় না মনে
E: The heart wants nothing else

T: Gaan chhara gaan chhara
B: গান ছাড়া গান ছাড়া
E: Nothing but song, nothing but song

[Verse 1]
T: Gaan e bondhure daki
B: গানে বন্ধুরে ডাকি
E: I call to my beloved through song

T: Gaan e premer chhobi aaki
B: গানে প্রেমের ছবি আঁকি
E: I paint the picture of love through song

T: Pabo bole asha rakhi
B: পাবো বলে আশা রাখি
E: I hold onto hope that I will find him

T: Na paile jabo mara
B: না পাইলে যাবো মারা
E: If I don't, I will surely die

[Verse 2]
T: Gaan amar jopo mala
B: গান আমার জপমালা
E: Song is my prayer beads

T: Gaan e khule premer tala
B: গানে খুলে প্রেমের তালা
E: Song unlocks the lock of love

T: Pran bondhu chikon-kala
B: প্রাণ বন্ধু চিকন-কালা
E: My soul's beloved, the slender dark one

T: Ontore daay ishara
B: অন্তরে দেয় ইশারা
E: Gives a sign from within my heart

[Verse 3]
T: Bhabe Karim dinohin
B: ভাবে করিম দীনহীন
E: Wretched Karim reflects

T: Ashbe ki aar shubho din
B: আসবে কি আর শুভ দিন
E: Will good days ever come again?

T: Jol chhara ki bachibe meen
B: জল ছাড়া কি বাঁচিবে মীন
E: Can a fish survive without water?

T: Duble ki bhashe mora
B: ডুবলে কি ভাসে মড়া
E: When submerged, does a corpse float?

[Glossary]
gaan | গান | song — for Abdul Karim, song is not entertainment but spiritual practice; the medium through which the divine is sought and loved
pagolpara | পাগলপারা | wildly restless, beyond madness — "pagol" (mad) + "para" (surpassing); the mind is helplessly uncontrollable without song
jopo mala | জপমালা | prayer beads, rosary — the string used to count repetitions of the divine name; here song itself becomes the act of prayer
premer tala | প্রেমের তালা | the lock of love — the heart is locked; song is the key that opens it
pran bondhu | প্রাণ বন্ধু | soul's beloved, friend of the heart — an intimate devotional address for the divine
chikon-kala | চিকন-কালা | the slender dark one — a beloved folk epithet for Krishna; "chikon" = slender and graceful, "kala" = dark
ishara | ইশারা | a sign, a signal — the divine speaks not in words but in subtle inner signs felt in the heart
dinohin | দীনহীন | wretched, lowly, destitute — Karim's humble self-description in the closing verse, the poet's signature of surrender
shubho din | শুভ দিন | auspicious day, good times — the longed-for day of union with the beloved
meen | মীন | fish — the Baul and Sufi image par excellence: the soul is a fish; without water (the divine), it cannot live even a moment
mora | মড়া | a corpse — the dark rhetorical question: without the divine, the seeker is already dead; the song is the only thing keeping him afloat
