[Glossary]
murshid | মুর্শিদ | Sufi spiritual guide — the one who illuminates the path to the divine
dhon | ধন | treasure — used here as a term of deep longing and affection
maya jaal | মায়া জাল | net of illusion — maya is worldly attachment; jaal is a net or trap
tantra-mantra | তন্ত্র-মন্ত্র | tantric ritual and sacred formula — external religious practices the poet finds insufficient
shastro-grontho | শাস্ত্র-গ্রন্থ | scripture and sacred texts — book-learning that cannot reach the divine
sagore | সাগরে | in the ocean — the divine is imagined as a limitless ocean in which the soul floats
ontore | অন্তরে | within, in the heart — the true dwelling place of the divine
andhokare | অন্ধকারে | in darkness — the seeker's despair of not finding the light
notoshire | নতোশিরে | with bowed head — a gesture of total humility and surrender
karojure | করজুড়ে | with folded hands — the Bengali gesture of respectful supplication
dorbare | দরবারে | at the court — the devotional court of the spiritual master
bhakter odhin | ভক্তের অধীন | bound to the devotee — the paradox that the divine becomes subservient to pure love
