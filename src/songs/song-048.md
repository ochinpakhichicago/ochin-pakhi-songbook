[Glossary]
rai | রাই | Rai — a beloved name for Radha; short form of Radharani, the eternal consort of Krishna
prabhati | প্রভাতী | dawn song — a devotional song sung at daybreak to awaken the deity; the Vaishnava tradition has a rich repertoire of these morning invocations
shyam | শ্যাম | the dark-complexioned one — a primary epithet of Krishna, evoking the dark monsoon cloud
monmohini | মনমোহিনী | she who enchants the mind — an epithet of Radha; captivating and mind-stealing
binodini | বিনোদিনী | the joyful, playful one — another epithet of Radha, evoking her delight and playfulness
nishi | নিশি | the night — the night of the divine couple's union is over; dawn calls the world back to worship
joyo Radhe | জয় রাধে | victory to Radhe — a devotional salutation; "jaya" = victory; a cry of reverence
kulkolonk | কুলকলঙ্ক | the stain upon family honour — Radha's love for Krishna was illicit in worldly eyes; her "kul" (family reputation) was at stake; in Vaishnava theology this social shame is transcended by divine love
basi phool | বাসি ফুল | stale flowers — flowers from the previous day's worship, to be cast into water and replaced with fresh ones for morning puja
jugol | যুগল | the divine couple — Radha and Krishna together; "jugol" worship (yugala-upasana) is the central practice of Bengali Vaishnavism
sebar dasi | সেবার দাসী | handmaidens of service — the sakhis (companions and attendants) of Radha who serve the divine couple; in Vaishnava devotion, the ideal is to be one of these handmaidens

[Discussion.Summary]
A Prabhati — a dawn song from the Vaishnava tradition — sung to awaken Radha from her night with Krishna. The Prabhati is one of the daily liturgical forms: sung at daybreak, it dramatises the moment when the sakhis (handmaidens) must rouse Radha so the divine couple can be seen and worshipped before the world stirs. The song is both practical (wake her, discard the old flowers, bring fresh ones) and gently teasing (have you no fear of your reputation?). At its heart it is a meditation on divine love that transcends all worldly honour.
