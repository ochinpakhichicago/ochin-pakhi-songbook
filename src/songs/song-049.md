[Glossary]
opar hoye | ওপার হয়ে | stranded on the far shore — the seeker is separated from the divine shore, helpless without a means to cross
dayamoy | দয়াময় | the Compassionate One — an epithet of the divine; the one in whom mercy resides
pare loye jao | পারে লয়ে যাও | take me across to the other shore — the central plea; "par" = the far shore of liberation
ghate | ঘাটে | at the ghat — the landing steps at a river; the place of crossing and departure; to remain at the ghat is to be stranded
bhanu | ভানু | the sun — a poetic name for the sun; "the sun has taken its seat" means the day is passing, time is running out
toma bine | তোমা বিনে | without you — complete helplessness in the absence of the divine
ghowr sankawte | ঘোর সংকটে | in terrible straits — "ghor" = dark, severe; "sankat" = crisis; an extremity of difficulty
bhajan sadhon | ভজন সাধন | devotion and spiritual practice — Lalon admits to having none; this is why he needs grace, not merit
kupothe gawmon | কুপথে গমন | walking the wrong path — a lifetime of spiritual misdirection; the fallen condition that makes the Redeemer's title so relevant
powtit-pabon | পতিত-পাবন | Redeemer of the fallen — "patit" = fallen, sinful; "paban" = purifier; a well-known divine epithet; Lalon uses the name as a claim on mercy
dohai | দোহাই | a cry for help, an appeal — invoking someone's name as a plea; calling upon the divine for urgent rescue
awgowtir goti | অগতির গতি | passage for the helpless — "agati" = one with nowhere to go; "gati" = movement, refuge, a way forward
awkhyati | অখ্যাতি | blame, ill-repute — Lalon boldly turns the argument: if you don't help the helpless, the stain falls on your own name
okuler powti | অকূলের পতি | Lord of the shoreless sea — "akul" = without shore, boundless; the divine as master of the infinite ocean, the only one capable of rescue
fokir Lalon | ফকির লালন | Fakir Lalon — Lalon's self-signature; "fakir" = wandering mendicant, the lowest and most dependent; he signs as the one with the least claim and the greatest need

[Arrangement]
Cello drone. Dotara intro. Subho enters. On 4 chord accordion enters.

[Discussion.Summary]
A Lalon song of pure supplication — the seeker stranded on the wrong shore, calling out to the Compassionate One to carry him across. Lalon makes no claim of merit: he has no devotion, no practice, and has walked the wrong path his whole life. His only argument is the divine's own name — "Redeemer of the fallen" — which he turns into a demand: if you do not redeem the fallen, the name is a lie and the blame is yours. The song closes with Lalon's characteristic signature as "Fakir" — the most dependent, the most in need, the one with the greatest claim on mercy precisely because he has none of his own.
