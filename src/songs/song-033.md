[Refrain]
T: Din furailo, hori hori bolo,
B: দিন ফুরাইলো, হরি হরি বলো,
E: The day is done — say Hari, say Hari

T: Manob jonom tor, gelo furaiya re.
B: মানব জনম তোর, গেলো ফুরাইয়া রে।
E: O, your human life is spent, it is gone

[Verse 1]
T: Ki kajo korite aili
B: কি কাজ করিতে আইলি

T: Ee bhobo songsarer majhe (mon mon re)
B: এই ভবো সংসারের মাঝে (মন মন রে)
E: In the midst of this mortal world (O my mind, my mind)

T: Taka poisa sona dana
B: টাকা পয়সা সোনা দানা
E: Money, coins, gold and jewels

T: Morle to ar songe jaina
B: মরলে তো আর সঙ্গে যায় না

B: ভেবে দেখো মন রে।

[Glossary]
din furailo | দিন ফুরাইলো | the day is done — the day of human life is over; an urgent alarm: time has run out
hori hori bolo | হরি হরি বলো | say Hari, say Hari — the call to take the divine name at all moments and especially at the moment of death; Hari is an epithet of Vishnu/Krishna
manob jonom | মানব জনম | human birth — the precious human life, now spent and gone
gelo furaiya | গেলো ফুরাইয়া | it is gone, spent, exhausted — the life that could have been used for devotion has run out
bhobo sonshar | ভবো সংসার | this mortal world — "bhob" = the world of becoming and existence; "sonshar" = the entanglement of worldly life
taka poisa sona dana | টাকা পয়সা সোনা দানা | money, coins, gold and jewels — all accumulated wealth of a lifetime; none of it travels with you
chheNra tyana | ছেঁড়া ত্যানা | a torn rag — at the moment of leaving, all worldly wealth is reduced to this; the final garment or simply nothing
apon | আপন | one's own — those called intimate and belonging; Lalon's point is that this sense of belonging is an illusion
stri putro dara suto | স্ত্রী পুত্র দারা সুত | wife and son, spouse and child — the intimate bonds of family; they dissolve the moment you die
chhokh bujilei | চোখ বুজিলেই | the moment the eyes close — at death; the instant the eyes shut, all bonds vanish
bhola mon | ভোলা মন | the forgetful mind — the mind that forgets its spiritual purpose and loses itself in the world
ocheton | অচেতন | unconscious, unaware — spiritually asleep; not awake to the truth of impermanence and the urgency of devotion
kandari | কাণ্ডারি | helmsman, navigator — the one who steers the boat; the Guru as guide across the ocean of existence
guru | গুরু | the spiritual teacher — in Baul tradition, the living Guru is indispensable; without a guide there is no path across

[Arrangement]

Molly and Sam instrumental: one time through. 2nd khamok.
End: Din furailo 3X — all sing "gelo furaiya re", hold out last time. No "Ki kajo" reprise.

[Discussion.Summary]
A Lalon song of the "akheri chetan" type — songs about the final awakening, the last reckoning at the end of life. The refrain ("the day is done — say Hari") lands as an alarm: time has run out, and the human life meant for devotion has been squandered. Three verses strip away every worldly attachment: wealth doesn't travel, family disappears the moment your eyes close, and you leave with nothing but a torn rag. The only answer Lalon offers is the Guru — the helmsman without whom the boat has no direction.
