[Glossary]
sohoj manush | সহজ মানুষ | the simple, natural human — "sohoj" = easy, natural, innate; in Baul philosophy this refers to the divine as it exists naturally within the living human; the "sahaja" (natural/innate) tradition sees the divine as immanent in ordinary humanity
bhoje dekh | ভজে দেখ | try worshipping, try practicing — Lalon's invitation is experiential, not dogmatic; he says try it and see for yourself
dibbo gyan | দিব্য জ্ঞান | divine understanding — the higher awareness through which the "simple human" is perceived as the seat of the divine
omullo nidhhi | অমূল্য নিধি | priceless treasure — the spiritual treasure found within the human; beyond all monetary calculation
bortomaan | বর্তমান | the present moment — the treasure is not in the afterlife but available right now in the living present
choron duti | চরণ দুটি | the two feet — to worship the feet is the deepest act of reverence in Bengali tradition; here it is the feet of the living human that deserve this worship
nitto bostu | নিত্য বস্তু | the eternal substance — the unchanging, permanent reality hidden within the changing world; Vedantic and Baul concept
maati | মাটি | earth, clay — after death the body returns to earth; find the truth now before the clay dissolves
bhed | ভেদ | the secret, the inner distinction — the esoteric knowledge; the hidden difference between appearance and reality that the Baul seeks to penetrate
behest khana | বেহেস্ত খানা | the food of paradise — "behest" = paradise (from Persian/Arabic "bahisht"); the pleasures of the afterlife promised by orthodox religion; Lalon is openly skeptical
nogod paona | নগদ পাওনা | cash in hand, what is due right now — contrasted with "baaki" (credit, deferred payment); Lalon uses a market metaphor: why trade present reality for a future promise?
salatul merajun | সালাতুল মেরাজুন | prayer is the mi'raj — from the hadith "al-salatu mi'raj al-mu'minin" (prayer is the ascent of believers); "miraj" = the Prophet's night ascent to heaven; Lalon cites this to show that the inner experience of prayer is the real ascent, not a physical afterlife journey
namaj | নামাজ | Islamic daily prayer — Lalon as a Baul saint transcends religious boundaries; he cites the inner meaning of namaz as an instance of the same universal inner practice
bishshashi | বিশ্বাসী | the faithful, the true believers — not a sectarian term for Lalon; it means sincere seekers of any tradition who truly see and hear
e bhubone | এ ভুবনে | in this world — not elsewhere, not after death; the world as it is right now is where the treasure lies

[Discussion.Summary]
A Lalon song of the "sahaj manush" tradition — the philosophy that the divine is most purely found in the living human being, not in scripture, ritual, or the paradise promised after death. The song makes three moves: first, an invitation to worship the simple human with divine eyes and find the priceless treasure in the present moment; second, a rejection of the afterlife bargain — you lose what is in your hand now for a credit you may never collect; third, a citation of the Islamic teaching that prayer is the mi'raj (ascent) — the inner experience of devotion is the true ascent, not an afterlife destination. Lalon synthesises Sufi, Vaishnava and Baul wisdom into one call: the treasure is here, now, in the person in front of you.
