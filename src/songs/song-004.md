[Glossary]
anaa | আনা | anna — an old currency unit, one-sixteenth of a rupee; six annas for the ferry, twelve wasted in total
sholo anaa | ষোলো আনা | sixteen annas — a full rupee; used figuratively to mean "complete" or "wholehearted"
ushul | উশুল | to recover, to recoup — the money spent on ferrying came to nothing
gaang | গাঙ | river — dialectal form of "nadi"; the wide river the lover must cross to reach the beloved
ghaeter maajhi | ঘাটের মাঝি | the ferryman at the ghat — the person who rows the boat at the river crossing
boitha | বৈঠা | oar — used to steer and row the boat
piriti | পিরিতি | love, longing, deep romantic attachment — a folk word for intense feeling
paan | পান | betel leaf — chewing betel reddens the lips; the act of preparing oneself to meet the beloved
shatar | সাঁতার | swimming — the narrator won't swim the river for fear of ruined clothes, a comic touch of vanity
nithur | নিঠুর | cruel, heartless — addressed to the absent beloved
kalaa | কালা | the dark one — a term of endearment for the beloved, often evoking Krishna's dark complexion
