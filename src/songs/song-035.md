[Refrain]
T: Somoy gele sadhon hobe na
B: সময় গেলে সাধন হবে না

B: দিন থাকিতে তিন এর সাধন

T: Keno janle na (tumi)
B: কেনো জানলে না (তুমি)

T: Jano na mon khale bile
B: জানো না মন খালে বিলে

[Glossary]
somoy gele sadhon hobe na | সময় গেলে সাধন হবে না | when the time passes practice cannot be done — the central teaching of the song; "sadhon" = spiritual practice, specifically the tantric-Baul body practice; it has a right time, and outside that time it cannot be performed; this is not an invitation to panic but a precise instruction: know the time
tin er sadhon | তিন এর সাধন | the practice of the three — "tin" = three; in Baul tantric physiology, the "three" refers to the three tidal moments of the body: the three significant phases that cycle through and create windows for practice; the specific knowledge of these three is gurupara-vidya (taught by the guru, not in texts)
khale bile | খালে বিলে | in channels and wetlands — "khal" = canal, channel; "bil" = low-lying wetland, oxbow lake; the natural landscape of rural Bengal; the image: the fish that lives in channels and wetlands has no choice when the water dries
meen | মীন | fish — the living creature entirely dependent on its medium; when water goes, the fish cannot survive; no dam built after the water has dried will bring the fish back
badhal | বাধাল | a dam, a barrier built to hold water — "badhal dile" = if you build a dam (now, after the water is gone); useless labour after the moment has passed; all the effort in the world cannot reconstruct what the right moment contained
mohona | মোহনা | the river mouth, the estuary — where the river meets the sea; in Bengali folk metaphor the mohona is also a place of confluence in the body, where channels meet; "mohona shukna thake" = the confluence stays dry; when the tidal moment is missed, even the great meeting-point dries up
osomoye krishi | অসময়ে কৃষি | farming out of season — "osomoye" = at the wrong time, unseasonably; farming that does not respect the season kills the farmer's effort and sometimes the farmer; the seed may even germinate (gachh jodio hoy bijer jore) but without the right season, no fruit comes
bijer jore | বীজের জোরে | by the seed's force — the sheer biological momentum of a seed can push a tree into existence even in the wrong season; but the tree remains fruitless; effort alone, force alone, cannot replace timing
Omabossay purnima hoy | অমাবস্যায় পূর্ণিমা হয় | at the new moon the full moon occurs — the great paradox; in the Baul tantric tradition this refers to the body's internal eclipse: the moment when the two inner channels (solar/lunar, or ida/pingala) meet and neutralize each other, creating a momentary state of stillness in the body's tidal rhythm; this is simultaneously "new moon" (all lunar energy absorbed) and "full moon" (everything radiant); it is called "Mohajog"
Mohajog | মহাযোগ | the great conjunction — "maha" = great; "yog/yoga" = conjunction, union, alignment; the astronomical/bodily moment when opposing forces align; in external astrology, a rare planetary conjunction; in Baul physiology, the brief window when the body's inner tides equalize and practice can penetrate; the most auspicious moment
se dine udoy | সে দিনে উদয় | on that day it rises — the Mohajog rises, appears; a window opens; but it does not stay open
dondow roy na tate | দণ্ডও রয় না তাতে | not even an instant remains — "dondo/danda" = a unit of time (24 minutes in Sanskrit measure); but here: not even a moment; the Mohajog-window is so brief that even a danda cannot be wasted; the practice must begin the instant the conjunction appears; this is why "somoy gele sadhon hobe na" — miss it by a breath and it is gone

[Discussion.Summary]
A compressed Lalon teaching-song: the right time for practice exists, it is brief, and when it passes nothing can recover it. The refrain announces the law — when the time goes, the practice cannot be done; while the day remains, do the practice of the three. Three natural images in the verses illustrate three kinds of missing the moment. Verse 1: the fish in the wetland — if the water dries before the fish moves, no dam built after will restore the water; the fish has no recourse. Verse 2: the farmer who farms out of season — the seed may be strong enough to push a tree up anyway, but the tree will not fruit; biological force cannot replace the season. Verse 3 is the key: at the new moon the full moon occurs — the Mohajog, the great conjunction — and the window opens. But not even an instant (dondow) remains in it before it closes. This third image shifts from the natural world to the body's internal tidal astronomy: the moment when the body's solar and lunar channels neutralize each other and the central channel opens. That is the window. That is the "tin er sadhon" — the practice of the three that must be known and caught at the right moment. Lalon gives no further instruction here — that is guru-vidya, given only in person. The song only says: know that the moment exists, know that it passes, know that the practice cannot be done without it.
