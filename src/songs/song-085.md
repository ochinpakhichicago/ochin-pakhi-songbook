[Refrain]
T: Guru dohai tomar monke amar
B: গুরু দোহাই তোমার মনকে আমার
E: Guru, I implore you — take this mind of mine

T: Lao go supothe
B: লাও গো সুপথে
E: Lead it on the right path

T: Tomar doya bine guru charon
B: তোমার দয়া বিনে গুরু চরণ
E: Without your grace, guru, your lotus feet —

T: Sadhbo ki mowte
B: সাধব কি মতে
E: How shall I seek in any way?

T: Lao go supothe
B: লাও গো সুপথে
E: Lead it on the right path

[Verse 1]
T: Tumi jare haogo sodoy
B: তুমি যারে হাও গো সদয়
E: Whoever you become gracious towards

T: Se tomare sadhone paay
B: সে তোমারে সাধনে পায়
E: They find you through their devotion

T: Bibadi taar swaboshe roy
B: বিবাদী তার স্বাবশে রয়
E: The inner adversary remains under their own sway

T: Tomar kripate
B: তোমার কৃপাতে
E: By your grace

[Verse 2]
T: Jontrote jontri jemon
B: যন্ত্রেতে যন্ত্রী যেমন
E: As the musician is to the instrument

T: Jemon bajay baje temon
B: যেমন বাজায় বাজে তেমন
E: As they play, so it sounds

T: Temni jontro amari mon
B: তেমনি যন্ত্র আমারি মন
E: Likewise, this mind of mine is the instrument

T: Bol tomar hate
B: বল তোমার হাতে
E: Say — it is in your hands

[Verse 3]
T: Jogai madhai dossu chhilo
B: জগাই মাধাই দস্যু ছিলো
E: Jagai and Madhai were once bandits

T: Tare gurur kripa holo
B: তারে গুরুর কৃপা হলো
E: Upon them the guru's grace descended

T: Odhin Lalon dohai dilo guru
B: অধীন লালন দোহাই দিলো গুরু
E: Helpless Lalon implores the guru

T: Sei ashate
B: সেই আশাতে
E: In that very hope

[Glossary]
dohai | দোহাই | I implore you — a fervent plea or invocation
supothe | সুপথে | on the right path, the good road
kripa | কৃপা | grace, divine compassion
bibadi | বিবাদী | adversary, opponent — here the enemy within oneself
jontro | যন্ত্র | instrument — here the mind is compared to a musical instrument
jontri | যন্ত্রী | musician, the one who plays the instrument
Jogai Madhai | জগাই মাধাই | two legendary sinners in Vaishnava tradition who were redeemed by Nityananda's grace — invoked here as proof that no one is beyond redemption
odhin | অধীন | helpless, subservient — Lalon's humble self-description

[Arrangement]
Khamok.
Sam alap to start, then subho slow until guru dohai rhythm comes in, only one chorus, don't sing tomar doya again.
Interludes: Nate, Molly, Sam.
Nate plays along with subhos melody last time (after cello solo).
After Sam solo go back to tomar doya.
Ending 3 times lao go supothe — rhythm cuts out at end of 2nd time. 3rd time acapella with a flute descending line and khamok drone.
