[Glossary]
laal pahari | লাল পাহাড়ি | red hills — the laterite hills of Birbhum and Purulia in West Bengal, famous for their deep red soil
rangamati | রাঙামাটি | coloured earth — the distinctive red laterite soil of the region
manai chhinai | মানাই ছিনাই | no restraint — a dialectal expression meaning nothing holds you back, complete freedom
ikkebare | ইক্কেবারে | completely, entirely — a dialectal intensifier common in Birbhum folk speech
sethak | সেথাক | there — dialectal form of "sekhane"
hariya | হারিয়ার | of the Hari community — an adivasi community of the region renowned for their folk music
madol | মাদল | a two-headed barrel drum — central to the folk and tribal music of this region
nagor | নাগর | beloved, lover — an affectionate address
shimul | শিমুল | the silk-cotton tree (Bombax ceiba) — blooms with vivid red flowers in spring
bhadu | ভাদু | a folk goddess and festival — celebrated in the Bhadra month, centred on the legend of a beloved local girl named Bhadu
bhadoro-ashino | ভাদর-আশিন | Bengali calendar months (August–October) — the season of Bhadu songs
kalo meye | কালো মেয়ে | dark-skinned girl — in Bengali folk tradition, dark skin is celebrated as an earthy, natural beauty
mala | মালা | garland — exchanging garlands symbolises love and betrothal in Bengali folk culture
baper beta | বাপের বেটা | son of a father — used here with irony: "what kind of man calls himself a man"
