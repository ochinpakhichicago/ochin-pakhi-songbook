[Refrain]
T: Tomar ghore bosot kore koyjona
B: তোমার ঘরে বসত করে কয়জনা
E: How many dwell within your house

T: Mon janona
B: মন জানো না
E: The mind does not know

[Verse 1]
T: Tomar ghore bas kore kara
B: তোমার ঘরে বাস করে কারা
E: Who is it that lives within your house

T: O mon janona
B: ও মন জানো না
E: O mind, you do not know

[Verse 2]
T: Ek jone chobi akhe ekmone o mon
B: এক জনে ছবি আঁকে একমনে ও মন
E: One of them paints a picture with full concentration, O mind

T: Ar ek jone bose bose rong makhe
B: আর এক জনে বসে বসে রং মাখে
E: And another sits and spreads colour over it

T: Oi abar sei chobi khan
B: ওই আবার সেই ছবি খান
E: And then again that very picture

T: Nosto kore kun jona kun jona
B: নষ্ট করে কোন জনা কোন জনা
E: Is ruined — by which one, which one?

[Verse 3]
T: Ekjone sur tole ek tare o mon
B: এক জনে সুর তোলে এক তারে ও মন
E: One raises a melody on a single string, O mind

T: Ar ekjone mondirate tal tole o mon
B: আর এক জনে মন্দিরাতে তাল তোলে ও মন
E: And another keeps rhythm on the mandira, O mind

T: Oi abar besura sur dore dekho
B: ওই আবার বেসুরা সুর ধরে দেখো
E: And look — another catches an out-of-tune note

T: Kun jona kun jona
B: কোন জনা কোন জনা
E: Which one, which one?

[Verse 4]
T: Rosh khaiya hoiya matal
B: রস খাইয়া হইয়া মাতাল
E: Getting drunk on the divine nectar

T: Oi dekho hat foske jay ghorar lagam
B: ওই দেখো হাত ফসকে যায় ঘোড়ার লাগাম

T: Sei lagam khana dhore dekho
B: সেই লাগাম খানা ধরে দেখো
E: Who is it that grabs hold of those reins — look

T: Kun jona kun jona
B: কোন জনা কোন জনা
E: Which one, which one?

[Glossary]
ghore bosot kore | ঘরে বসত করে | dwells within the house — the "house" (ghar) is the human body in Baul philosophy; Lalon asks who the multiple tenants of this body-house are
koy jona | কয় জনা | how many persons — the central mystery: the Baul tradition speaks of several inner forces or selves that inhabit the body simultaneously
chobi akhe | ছবি আঁকে | paints a picture — one inner force creates; another colours; a third destroys; the self is a site of competing creative and destructive drives
rong makhe | রং মাখে | spreads colour — the force that adorns and embellishes what has been made
nosto kore | নষ্ট করে | ruins, destroys — the destructive inner force that undoes the work of the others; the ego, or the lower self
sur tole | সুর তোলে | raises a melody — the creative musical force; one inner self attempts harmony
mandira | মন্দিরা | small finger cymbals — used to keep tala (rhythmic cycle) in devotional music; here another self keeps the beat
besura | বেসুরা | out of tune, discordant — the inner force that disrupts harmony; the self that cannot keep the melody
rosh | রস | juice, nectar, rasa — in Baul philosophy, the divine essence or nectar within the body; becoming drunk on rosh is spiritual intoxication
matal | মাতাল | drunk, intoxicated — overcome by the divine rasa; spiritual ecstasy that loosens the grip on the reins of the self
lagam | লাগাম | reins, bridle — the means of controlling a horse; here the instrument of control over the body and the senses; who holds the reins of this body-horse?
kun jona | কোন জনা | which one — the haunting refrain; Lalon never names the dwellers, leaving the question open for the seeker to discover within
