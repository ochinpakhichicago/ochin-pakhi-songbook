[Refrain]
T: Emon manob jonom ar ki hobe?
B: এমন মানব জনম আর কি হবে?
E: Will such a human birth ever come again?

T: Mon ja koro twaray koro ei bhobe
B: মন যা করো ত্বরায় করো এই ভবে
E: O mind, whatever you must do, do it swiftly in this life

[Verse 1]
T: Anonto rup srishti korlen Saain
B: অনন্ত রূপ সৃষ্টি করলেন সাঁই
E: The Lord created countless forms of existence

T: Shuni manober uttom kichhui naai
B: শুনি মানবের উত্তম কিছুই নাই
E: Yet I hear that nothing surpasses the human form

T: Koto deb-debotagon kore aradhon
B: কতো দেব-দেবতাগণ করে আরাধন
E: How many gods and goddesses bow in worship

T: Jonmo nite manobe
B: জন্ম নিতে মানবে
E: Longing to be born as human

[Verse 2]
T: Koto bhaggeyer phole na jani (mon re)
B: কতো ভাগ্যের ফলে না জানি (মন রে)
E: Through what great fortune, I cannot know (O my mind)

T: Peyechho ei manob toroni
B: পেয়েছো এই মানব তরণী

T: (Tori) Beye jao twaray

[Verse 3]

[Glossary]
manob jonom | মানব জনম | human birth — in Baul and Hindu philosophy, human birth is the rarest and most precious of all forms of existence; the soul passes through countless lifetimes to attain it
bhobe | ভবে | in this life, in this world — the transient material world; the present incarnation, the only window in which to act
twaray | ত্বরায় | swiftly, urgently — do not delay; the span of human life is finite and may not return
saain | সাঁই | the Lord — the Baul term for the formless universal divine, used throughout Lalon's songs
manober uttom | মানবের উত্তম | highest of all is the human — human consciousness is understood as the pinnacle of all created forms in creation
deb-debotagon | দেব-দেবতাগণ | gods and goddesses — in the Baul view, even celestial beings long to be reborn human, because only humans can pursue liberation
manob toroni | মানব তরণী | the boat of human birth — the body-as-boat is a central Baul image; you must row it across the ocean of existence while you have the chance
sudharay | সুধারায় | in the current, in the stream — the flow of time and worldly life; the current that can swallow the boat if one is not alert
madhurjyo bhajan | মাধুর্য্য ভজন | the sweetest devotion — the deepest quality of loving worship; only the human form is capable of this depth of devotional love
nironjon | নিরঞ্জন | the Spotless One — an epithet for the formless divine, free of all blemish or attribute; it is this formless one who shaped the human form for devotion
kinar | কিনার | shore, bank — the far shore of liberation; to miss this crossing is to remain in the cycle of birth and death
odhin Lalon | অধীন লালন | dependent Lalon — Lalon's signature of humility in the closing verse; he places himself as a servant, bound and subject to the divine

[Discussion.Summary]
A foundational Lalon song on the supreme preciousness of human birth. The central argument — that even gods envy the human form because only humans can perform genuine devotion and attain liberation — is one of the core ideas of Baul and Hindu philosophy. Lalon frames the urgency through the "boat of human birth" metaphor: you have received this vessel through extraordinary fortune; row it swiftly before it founders. The song closes with Lalon's characteristic humility: "dependent Lalon reflects on this."
