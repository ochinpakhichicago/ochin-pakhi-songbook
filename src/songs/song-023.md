[Refrain]

[Glossary]
sokar belar sur | শোকের বেলার সুর | the melody of the hour of grief — "shok" = grief, mourning; "bela" = time of day, hour; "sur" = melody, musical note; you are not the melody of joy or celebration but the specific melody that belongs to sorrow; a piece of music that fits only the grieving hour
hridoy olos udas | হৃদয় অলস উদাস | making the heart languid and hollow — "olos" = languid, heavy with inertia, unable to move; "udas" = melancholic, hollow, detached; not depression but a tender heaviness; the beloved's presence induces this state — not despair but a stilled, suspended grief
oshru bharatur | অশ্রু ভরাতুর | heavy with tears — "oshru" = tears; "bharatur" = filled to overflow, heavy with; the music (or the beloved) is saturated with tears; not weeping but brimming
bhorer tara | ভোরের তারা | the morning star — the star (planet Venus) that shines in the east just before dawn and then disappears when the sun rises; a thing of singular cold beauty that belongs to the transitional moment; its appearance signals both the beauty of pre-dawn and the approaching loss of night; it is always already leaving
sajolo chaowa | সজল চাওয়া | a wet gaze, eyes not quite dry — "sajolo" = with water, moist; "chaowa" = looking, gaze; the beloved's eyes as they look carry moisture — not flowing tears but eyes that hold tears back; the gaze of someone on the edge of weeping
bhalobasar cheye kanna paowa | ভালোবাসার চেয়ে কান্না পাওয়া | receiving tears more than love — what the beloved's gaze gives is not warmth but tears; the relationship exceeds love by producing this tenderness-that-weeps; more powerful than love, and more sorrowful
ratri seshe chad | রাত্রি শেষে চাঁদ | the moon at night's end — the moon as it sets at dawn; having lit the night, it disappears just as day comes; beautiful in its departure precisely because the departure is certain; "bidayo bidhur" = sorrowful in farewell
bidayo bidhur | বিদায় বিধুর | mournful in departure — "bidayo" = farewell, leave-taking; "bidhur" = sorrowful, aching; not the departure of anger but the departure of beauty that must leave; the moon does not choose to go — it simply must
bhorer jhora phul | ভোরের ঝরা ফুল | the flower fallen at dawn — a flower that bloomed in the night (night-blooming, like the night-queen, shiuli, or chameli) and has fallen by morning; dew-drenched, still fresh, white and pure — but its moment is done; it lies on the ground, worthy of being placed on an altar
shishir bheja subhro suchi | শিশির ভেজা শুভ্র শুচি | dew-drenched, white, pure — the three qualities of the fallen dawn flower: wet with dew (not artificial moisture but the night's own gift), white (subhro = brilliant white, luminous), pure (suchi = ritually clean, untouched)
pujarinir tul | পূজারিণীর তুল | worthy of a devotee-woman's offering — "pujarini" = a woman who performs puja, who makes ritual offerings; "tul" = comparison, match, worthy of being; the fallen flower is exactly what a devotee would gather to place before the deity; the beloved has the quality of sacred offering
orun | অরুণ | the reddish dawn light — "arun" = the personification of dawn; the reddish-gold light just before sunrise; also the charioteer of the sun; here used as an adjective of quality: dawn-like, suffused with that particular tender redness
torun | তরুণ | young — "tarun" = young, youthful, fresh; paired with "orun" (dawn): you are the dawn, you are young; both words share a quality of being at the beginning, not yet arrived at fullness
karun | করুণ | tender, compassionate, gentle — "karun" = pitifully tender, full of compassion; "karun taro cheye" = more tender than that even; the final quality exceeds the others; beyond being young and dawn-like, the beloved is suffused with a tenderness that borders on the pitiful
bishad loker meye | বিষাদ লোকের মেয়ে | the daughter of the world of sorrow — "bishad" = sorrow, melancholy, sadness; "lok" = world, realm; as if the beloved were born from a country of sorrow; she smiles ("hasir dekhe") but her origin is grief; the smile is worn over the sadness, not replacing it
Indra sobha | ইন্দ্র সভা | Indra's court — the divine court of the king of the gods; a place of continuous music, dance, and light; apsaras (celestial dancers) perform; instruments sound; the place of divine celebration; everything plays there
mouno bina | মৌন বীণা | the silent vina — the vina (veena, a stringed instrument) that is present in the divine court but is not sounding; present in the hall of music but itself mute; a lute that carries music in its form but does not play
nirobo nupur | নীরব নূপুর | the quiet anklet — "nupur" = ghunghroo, ankle bells worn by dancers; in a dancer's court they ring with every step; but this one is silent; the dancer who is not dancing, the music that is not sounding; the beloved is the beautiful instrument of silence in the court of all music

[Discussion.Summary]
A Nazrulgeeti of sustained melancholy beauty — a portrait of the beloved as the embodiment of a particular kind of tender sorrow. The opening states the paradox plainly: you are my melody, but a melody of grief — not celebration. You make the heart languid and heavy, suffused with tears. The song then builds a series of images, each capturing the same quality from a different angle. The morning star: your gaze is like it — wet, singular, already leaving. The departing moon: you are the moon at night's end, mournful in farewell. The flower fallen at dawn: dew-drenched, white, pure, no longer on the branch, worthy of an altar. Then the third verse reaches its series: you are the dawn (orun), you are young (torun), and more tender than either of those — more karun. You appear to be smiling but you are the daughter of sorrow. And finally: in the court of all music (Indra's divine court), you are the instrument that is not sounding — the silent vina, the quiet anklet. Nazrul's genius in this song is to make the silence and the stillness and the leaving feel not like deprivation but like a kind of supreme beauty. The beloved is not absent; she is the presence that evokes this specific quality of grief-tinged tenderness that only certain music can carry.
