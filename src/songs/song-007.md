[Glossary]
moner manush | মনের মানুষ | the person of the heart — Lalon's central concept; the divine presence dwelling within the self, the inner beloved one spends a lifetime seeking
milon | মিলন | union, meeting — the longed-for moment of merging with the divine inner self
chatokopray | চাতকপ্রায় | like the chatak bird — the chatak (pied cuckoo) is said to drink only rainwater; it gazes at the sky in single-minded longing, a classic image of devotional waiting
aharnishi | অহর্নিশি | day and night, ceaselessly — from "aho" (day) + "nishi" (night)
kaalo-shashi | কালো-শশী | the dark moon — the new moon, dark and invisible; here the longing self that gazes into the dark searching for the hidden beloved
charon daasi | চরণদাসী | servant at the feet — the humblest form of devotional surrender, to serve at the beloved's feet
kopalgune | কপালগুণে | by fate — "kopal" means forehead; in Bengali tradition one's fate is written on the forehead at birth
bidyut | বিদ্যুৎ | lightning — the sudden flash of divine vision or presence that appears and vanishes; the divine is like lightning hidden in cloud
anweson | অন্বেষণ | searching, seeking — the lover's relentless quest for the hidden divine
kala | কালা | the dark one — the inner divine beloved, dark like a monsoon cloud; evokes Krishna's dark complexion
darpane | দর্পণে | in the mirror — the soul itself is the mirror in which the divine form is glimpsed
rup | রূপ | form, beauty — the divine form seen in moments of contemplation and remembrance
smrane | স্মরণে | in remembrance — the act of recalling the divine form; memory as spiritual practice
lok lojja | লোক লজ্জা | public shame — the social embarrassment the true lover transcends; when remembrance of the beloved comes, no worldly judgment matters
prem | প্রেম | love — divine love as direct knowledge; Lalon's final word: only the one who loves truly knows what love is
