[Glossary]
chal mini | চল মিনি | come Mini, let us go — "Mini" is the wife; the invitation is tinged with desperation rather than hope; the homeland has nothing left to offer
cha bagan | চা বাগান | tea garden, tea estate — the plantation system of Assam where Bengali, Sylheti and other migrant workers were brought under the arkatti labour contractor system
kor mara | কোড় মারা | Digging the soil with a spade but here beaten with a whip
pata tula | পাতা তুলা | plucking tea leaves — the primary labour of tea garden workers, done mostly by women in harsh conditions
faaNki diya | ফাঁকি দিয়া | by deceiving, by tricking — the arkatti system relied on labour contractors who recruited workers from villages with false promises of good wages and conditions
Jodurom | যদুরাম | a personal name — represents the arkatti, the labour contractor who lured poor villagers to Assam; the target of the song's bitter address
babu | বাবু | the Indian clerk or manager — the lower-tier supervisor in the plantation hierarchy, demanding work from the labourers
sordar | সরদার | the sardar, the headman — the gang leader; responsible for keeping the workers in line and delivering them when required
saheb | সাহেব | the European plantation owner or manager — at the top of the hierarchy; the threat of "taking the skin off their backs" refers to the physical punishment routine under the plantation system
pither cham | পিঠের চাম | the skin off the back — a brutal idiom for flogging; corporal punishment was a legal instrument of the plantation system until well into the 20th century
puti machh | পুঁটি মাছ | tiny freshwater fish — one of the smallest and cheapest; here symbolising the near-starvation diet of the plantation worker
kyalagular tel | কেয়াগোলার তেল | oil from the storehouses of Hindi-speaking businessmen
jhul | ঝুল  | broth, thin curry sauce — the thinnest form of a fish preparation; a few drops of broth to make the tiny fish stretch further
dihi dihi | ডিহি ডিহি | give give — dialectal form (Sylheti/regional) of "de de"; the child's hungry cry
muroli | মুরলি | a flute — associated with Krishna; here the sound of the village and the life left behind; the longing for home

[Discussion.Summary]
A song by Kali Dasgupta about the systematic exploitation of Bengali migrant workers in the Assam tea gardens. It follows the whole arc: the arkatti contractor (Jodurom) who lures workers from the desperate villages with false promises; the brutal plantation hierarchy of babu, sardar and saheb; the threat of physical punishment; and the starvation diet — a one-paisa puti fish, rancid oil, a little broth. "Mini" is the wife pulled into this world. The song ends with the child crying for water and the sound of the murali drifting back from the village that no longer exists for them.
